Licensing

This version of the certificate is designed to be used within the UK. Please contact us if you want to use the certificate in other countries.

Central government includes all government departments and arms-length bodies. It does not include local government. Data that is collected and maintained by central government is owned by the Crown, which means it must be published as open data under the Open Government Licence unless the publisher has an exemption from the Office of Public Sector Information.
Data that is collected and maintained by UK central government is owned by the Crown, which means it must be published as open data under the Open Government Licence unless the publisher has an exemption from the Office of Public Sector Information.
Choose the open licence that you are using to make available your creative content for other people to use. Note that creative content does not include facts.
In Europe, you need to have a licence that covers your database rights as well as any copyright for creative content in the data. Your database rights cover the way in which you structure the data you make available.

Unless you have been granted an exemption from OPSI, if you are UK central government then you must publish your content and data using the Open Government Licence.

Ownership

You only have the right to publish open data if you own the data, or if you have been granted permission to republish data owned by someone else.

The open data certificate demonstrates that the source dataset is available as open data.
Give the name of the other organisation that owns the data.
Give the website of the other organisation that owns the data.
